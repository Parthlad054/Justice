# Kanoon Sahayak (LawEasy India) — Project Documentation

> A simple, bilingual (Hindi + English) legal awareness web app that helps everyday Indian citizens understand their rights, know what to do in common situations, and confidently stand up when someone breaks the rules.

---

## 1. Project Overview

**Goal:** Make Indian law understandable and actionable for the common person — no legal jargon, no confusion. Help people know their rights, follow correct procedure, and push back (legally and calmly) when someone violates their rights or breaks a rule.

**Core Value Proposition:**
- Learn law in plain Hindi + English
- Know exactly what to do, step by step
- Be able to *cite* the correct Act/Section when challenging wrong behavior
- Access ready-made complaint templates instead of feeling helpless

**Important design principle:** The app informs and empowers — it never encourages confrontation, vigilante action, or unverified accusations. Every "challenge" feature should push the user toward the correct *official* channel (police, consumer forum, RTI, labour office) rather than a direct personal confrontation with strangers.

## 7. New Feature Ideas — "Know Your Rights, Challenge the Wrong" 🚀

These features turn the app from an "information library" into a tool people can actually *use* in the moment.

### A. Empowerment Tools
1. **Rights Card Generator** — Pick a situation (e.g. "traffic stop", "landlord dispute"), and the app generates a shareable/printable card stating: *"Under [Act/Section], I have the right to ___."* Great for showing calmly to the other party.
2. **Complaint Letter Generator** — Auto-fills templates (consumer complaint, RTI application, labour complaint, police complaint) with the user's details — download as PDF or copy text.
3. **Fine/Penalty Calculator** — Enter violation type → see the legal fine range, so users know if they're being overcharged.
4. **"What To Say" Scripts** — Short, polite scripts for common confrontations (e.g., "How to respond if a police officer asks for a bribe" or "How to respond if a shopkeeper refuses a bill").

### B. Reporting & Escalation
5. **Nearest Authority Finder** — Directory of local police stations, consumer forums, labour offices, women's helplines, cyber cells (state-wise, manually curated JSON to start).
6. **Escalation Ladder Guide** — For each law topic, show a visual "if step 1 fails, do step 2" flow (e.g., Seller → Consumer Forum → State Commission → National Commission).
7. **Helpline Directory** — One-tap-call list of key national helplines (Police 112, Women 181, Cyber Crime 1930, Child 1098, Senior Citizen 14567).

### C. Learning & Engagement
8. **Daily Law Quiz** — Gamified 3-question quiz to build legal literacy, with streaks/badges.
9. **"Law of the Day" Push Notification** — Keeps users engaged (via PWA notifications).
10. **Real-Life Case Stories** — Short, anonymized story format: "Ramesh's landlord refused deposit — here's what he did and won."
11. **Community Q&A (Moderated)** — Users post situations, verified volunteers/law students answer (needs moderation to avoid misinformation).

### D. Trust & Safety Features
12. **Content Verification Badge** — Mark each law entry as "Reviewed by [volunteer/law student name]" with last-updated date, to build trust.
13. **Clear Disclaimers** — Every page reminds: *"This is general information, not legal advice. For serious matters, consult a lawyer or free legal aid (NALSA)."*
14. **Free Legal Aid Directory** — Link to District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) contacts for those who can't afford lawyers.
15. **Anti-Misuse Guardrails** — No feature should let users publicly "name and shame" individuals — always route to official reporting channels to avoid defamation/misuse.

### E. Accessibility & Reach
16. **Voice Search / Voice Explanation** — For low-literacy users, add text-to-speech in Hindi/English.
17. **Regional Language Expansion** — Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, etc. (Phase 2).
